Fly-Ball

OFFICIAL RULES OF FLY-BALL

Court/Play Area

Alright. This will be played on the basketball field until we find a better court. There will be 3 “zones”, like the image on the left. Each part of this three pointer line is a zone. (indicated by the white lines cutting the court into 3rds.) As you can see, there are zones 1, 2, and 3.

Equipment

As for the equipment of the game: You can only use a basketball. (which will be referred to from now as the “ball.”) This, however, might change in the future. You will need to run and use your hands. Your hands, in this game, will be defined as the area between your fingertips and wrist, and the back also counts. (This means that you can’t hit the ball with your fingertips.)

Goal/Object

The goal of the game is to make your opponent get out by making them get a fault, which will happen when they do one of these things:

> Let the ball bounce on their “zone” more than 1 time.
> Miss the ball. (This will only happen if the ball is not too high above them.)
> Hit the ball out of bounds or don’t hit the ball into the opposing player’s zone.

Setup

2 players will situate themselves in zones 1 and 3. The server will serve in zone 1. To visualize this, look back to the image in "Court/Play Area."

Serving

To serve, you can really do it however you like, as long as the ball goes to the other player’s side. When serving and after serving, players must remain in their respective zones until the other hits the ball, making it so that you can move freely.

Playing

When playing, players will hit the ball to the opponent’s zone, but they must let the ball touch the ground once until they can hit it. Anything else is counted as a fault. The players will continue to hit the ball to the other’s court, like a mesh between volleyball and four-square. Keep in mind that it is encouraged to move between zones. You cannot move to the zone that the other player is in, but you can move to any other zone. For example, if player A is in zone 2, and player B is in zone 3, player B can move to zone 1, but not zone 2, as player A is occupying it. However, you need to hit the ball before you move. If the other player is hitting the ball, or if the ball is coming towards you, you may not move, or you will get a fault. It is CRUCIAL that you hit the ball to your opponent’s side, or else you’ll get a fault. Ypu CANNOT hit the ball so that it doesn't reach the other's zone.

Faults/Losing

When you make someone “lose,”(see, “Play.”) they will gain a “fault.” Upon gaining 2 faults, a player has lost, and will go to the back of the line, where they will wait until they can go back in. When someone new goes into the court, they will go to zone 3, while the person who won will go to zone 1. You will get a fault for kicking or not using your hands to hit the ball.

Interference

Interference is when an object or person which is not the players or ground touches the ball, in which case the round will be restarted, and all faults will be reset. Keep in mind that if you are in line, you cannot touch the ball. Doing so is counted as interference.

Tips & Customization

The good thing about this game is that it’s ok if you modify it, as long as you tell the others about your modification or tweak. As for tips, you should remember to:

> Keep an eye on the ball.
> Hit up, not down, unless you're trying to bounce the ball on the ground.
> Move! If the ball is too close to bounce on the ground, or if it's out of reach, move!
> Remember that you can switch zones. If the ball is too far away, you can move the zone the ball is going to land into.
